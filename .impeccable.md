# Impeccable — Kindraw

## Design Context

### Users

Kindraw serves **small, async-first teams** (and the individuals within them) who think visually — sketching architecture, flows, diagrams, and ideas. The product is built around GitHub-authenticated identity, workspaces/folders, real-time collaboration rooms, and read-only public share links. People reach for it to _organize many drawings_, share a link, and occasionally co-draw live. The job to be done: capture and organize visual thinking fast, then share it without SaaS overhead. Secondary technical audience signals (Mermaid text-to-diagram, technical exports) exist but the framing is broader than "developers only" — it's collaborative visual thinking for small teams.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **calm, human, effortless.** The interface should feel like a quiet, generous space that gets out of the way so the canvas is the protagonist — while keeping a light, creative, slightly handmade warmth (echoing Excalidraw's hand-drawn Virgil canvas font). Emotional goals: **calm + focus** (room to think, no distraction) and **lightness + creativity** (inviting, humanistic, a touch playful). Never cold, corporate, or busy.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Keep and refine** the existing identity — do not reinvent. The current visual language is the source of truth:

- **Theme:** Light/warm chrome is the default and primary direction (coherent with the always-light canvas, daytime/clean feel). A **dark theme is a planned option** for night/long sessions — design new chrome with dark-mode viability in mind (use `light-dark()` / tokens, avoid hardcoding light-only values), but light is what ships first.
- **Brand color:** Purple `#6965DB` (darker `#5B57D1`, darkest `#4A47B1`, light `#E3E2FE`, hover `#5753D0`). Used sparingly as accent — delicate radial gradient washes, active states, primary actions — never as bold blocks. Accent is rare by design.
- **Neutrals (warm-leaning, tinted):** off-white `#FCFBFF`, cool white `#F7F8FC`, cream `#FFF8EF`, warm neutral `#F7F4EE`. Text: `#1F2937` (primary), `#132032` (dark navy for buttons/titles), `#405062` / `#5B6574` (secondary/meta). Borders: `rgba(31,41,55,0.06–0.10)`.
- **Status colors:** amber/orange (`#F4B75D`, text `#7F3E04`) for saving/badges; blue (`#609EFF`, text `#15407D`) for info/status; teal/green (`#10B981`/`#0F766E`) for live collaboration; red (`#B42318`) for errors.
- **Typography:** UI chrome uses **Assistant** (400/500/600/700). Canvas uses **Virgil** (hand-drawn) + Excalifont. Keep this split — the hand-drawn canvas font is part of the soul; chrome stays clean Assistant. Headings use a modular/fluid scale (`clamp(2rem, 4vw, 3rem)` for h1); section labels are small uppercase with `letter-spacing: 0.06–0.08em`.
- **Shape & depth:** soft rounded corners — `14px` (buttons), `20–24px` (cards/sections), `999px` (pills/avatars). Shadows are soft elevation (`0 16–18px 40–44px rgba(31,41,55,0.08)`), not hard drop shadows. Frosted glass (`backdrop-filter: blur(18px)`, semi-transparent surfaces) used purposefully for topbar/overlays — not slathered everywhere.
- **Gradients:** subtle warm→cool shell washes (warm `rgba(255,207,138,0.28)` top-left + cool `rgba(96,158,255,0.18)` top-right) and gentle linear card fades. Always low-opacity and atmospheric, never loud. No gradient text.
- **Spacing:** generous breathing room — `20px` section gaps, `18–28px` padding, slightly asymmetric sidebar padding (`24px` left / `18px` right). Cards don't touch edges.

**Anti-references:** generic AI-dashboard slop — colored left-border accent stripes, cyan-on-dark, purple→blue neon gradients, gradient text, card-in-card nesting, identical icon-topped card grids, everything-is-a-modal. Kindraw is warm and human, not a SaaS template.

### Design Principles

1. **The canvas is the protagonist.** Chrome recedes; it frames the work without competing with it. When in doubt, remove or quiet an element rather than add one.
2. **Warm, tinted, never sterile.** Neutrals lean warm and are tinted toward the brand purple; pure black/white never appear. Calm comes from soft surfaces and breathing room, not gray emptiness.
3. **Accent is rare on purpose.** Purple appears as occasional washes and key actions — its scarcity is what gives it power. 60% surface / 30% secondary / 10% accent by visual weight.
4. **Soft depth, purposeful glass.** Elevation via soft shadows and selective frosted overlays — to clarify layering, never as decoration.
5. **A handmade soul.** The hand-drawn canvas font and gentle warmth keep Kindraw human and a little playful; clean Assistant chrome keeps it legible and trustworthy. Hold both.
6. **Build dark-ready.** Light ships first, but new chrome should use tokens / `light-dark()` so the planned dark theme doesn't require a rewrite.
